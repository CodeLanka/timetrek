ATHUGALPURA - අැතුගල්පුර


Kurunegala or Athugalpura kingdom is one was the shortest eras of the Sri Lankan history. Though it was a short period of time, the literature was rich as many wise men went on writing Buddhist books and Sinhala literature and poem books. King Buwanekabahu II (1293-1302 A.D) made Kurunegala his kingdom and was advised by the Budhist monks. The king ruled for 7 years and his son, king Parakramabahu IV (1302-1326 A.D) became the next king.

The "Chakrawalaya" is a construction which is yet to be properly time dated. It is in Panduwasnuwara and some archeologists believe it to be from Polonnaruwa era and some assume it came from Kurunegala era.
Those who say it is from Kurunegala era say that this is supposed to represent the center of the Universe. But others believe that this is the pillar-like circular structure which princess Chithra who is also known as Unmada Chithra was imprisoned.
King Parakramabahu III ruled the Kurunegala kingdom for 24 years and he is known as wise and an kind king. The king built a chamber for the sacred tooth relic in Kurunegala and placed it there. He also made a book called "Dalada Siritha" which consists of methods of worshiping the tooth relic and maintainance of it. The king made a massive temple "Asgiriya" in the city of Kandy and named Kandy as "Senkadagala Puraya"
