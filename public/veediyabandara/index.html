Welcome to the Kingdom of Kotte.
1412 - 1597

The Kingdom of Kotte, centered on Sri Jayawardenepura Kotte, was a kingdom that flourished in Sri Lanka during the 15th century

After King Parakuramabahu the 6th, ascended the throne in 1415, he moved the capitol to Kotte.
He then ordered the prince "Sapumal" to go and conquer the nothern part of the country from "Araya Chakrawarthi" and take Jaffna into control. Prince "Ambulugala" was sent to the up country to counter "Jothiya" who ruled the up country at that time. The subsequent period is considered as a politically stable time period in Sri Lankan history. After king Parakramabahu VI's death, his grandson king "Jayabahu II"(1468 - 1472) inherited the throne. Prince Sapumal grabbed the throne by killing king "Jayabahu II" under the name "Buwanekabahu" the 6th(1472 - 1480). The next king was his son king "Parakramabahu VII"(1480 - 1484).

Thotagamuwe Sri Rahula Thera (1408 - 1491) was a Buddhist monk and an eminent scholar, who lived during the Kotte era.
He was a multi-linguist who was given the title "Shad Bhasha Parameshwara" due to his mastery in six oriental languages which prevailed in the Indian subcontinent.
Sangharaja Thotagamuwe Sri Rahula Thera was also a distinguished author, veteran astrologer and a proficient ayurvedic physician.

Veediya Bandara

One of the most decorated warriors of history, Veediya Bandara was a warrior who fought against the forces of Seethawaka as well as the Portugese. Veediye Bandara was able to protect the kingdom of Kotte from many invasions. Including a South-Indian backed invasion from Seethawaka, which not only he crused using his military expertise, he demamded that King Buwanekabahu ask the Seethawaka ruler for the heads of the South-Indian commanders, inflicting fear upon anyone who would even think of attacking Kotte.

Prince Ambulugala, under the name king "Veera Parakramabahu (VIII)" owns the throne by killing king Parakramabahu VII. He had five sons from two queens namely Buwanekabahu, Sri Rajasinghe, Vijayabahu from the chief queen and Sakakalavalla and Taniyavalla from the second queen.
