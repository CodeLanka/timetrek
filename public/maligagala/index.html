Where kings walked

MALIGA GALA

The invasion of "Kalinga Magha", the cruel Hindu dictator started in 1215 A.D. The invasion lead to a mass distruction of Buddhist temples, literature and mass murder of people in Sri Lanka. Since the "Magha" troops were ruling most of the North side of the island, the rest of the sinhalease people were forced to retreat to the south-west. Dambadeniya kingdom was established in a result of this. King Vijayabahu III (1215-1236 AD) was one of the popular provincial leaders who made Dambadeniya his kingdom.

The elder son of King Vijayabahu III, Parakramabahu II (1236-1270) went to rule the Dambadeniya kingdom after his father. It is said that the young prince was a great warrior and showing talents in many areas like literature and law. He was awarded an honour of literature for his creation of "Kavsilumina", a book of poems. The king defeated the evil ruler "Magha" and took over the country in to his hands. He then went on repairing the damage caused by "Magha" and establishing Buddhist temples and Stupas.


Maliga Gala - The Rock of the Palaces

During the days of the Dambadeniya kingdom, the kings used "Maliga Gala", a 600 ft tall rock as the ground for thier palaces. It's situated near the "Raja Maha Viharaya" temple and the ruins of the ponds and other buildings can be seen all around the place. The rock stair case, mid garden of the palace, Rathmal pond, cloud wall, Mawee pond and the great tunnel are some places that you should visit while visiting Dambadeniya.
