Kingdom of Yapahuwa
යාපහුව රාජධානිය

Welcome to Yapahuwa where the city is enriched with rain water and with many small and large tanks. The ancient ruins of Yapahuwa can be still seen with it's former glory. Yapahuwa acted as a fortress for many provincial leaders in times of Polonnaruwa and Dambadeniya because of it's unique placement.

King Buwanekabahu I (1272-1284 A.D)

King Buwanekabahu was the first king in Yapahuwa and he left the previous kingdom of Dambadeniya. The secure placement of Yapahuwa might be a reason why the king left Dambadeniya because of the continous invasions coming in. The king was a religous king and he brought the sacred tooth relic of Buddha from Plonnaruwa to Yapahuwa and placed it there. He also wrote the "Thripitaka" in "Pali" language and offered it to the main temples in Sri Lanka
