Ambekke
A pinnacle of woodcraft in the Gampola Era
A picture speaks a thousand words...and it carries some with it too
Ambekke is a place which one can truly see the Sinhelese art and craft in action. The almost pagan style of creating chimeras from known animals is used in the woodcraft. The Gaja-Sinha is a good example. There are numerous wooden sculptures to behold in this aincient place. The talen of the craftsmen must have been at very high levels since all four sides of the wooden pillars had to be carved flawlessly, otherwise a mistake would render the entire pillar useless.
But the most unique creation of these is the "Madol Kurupawa" or "Kenimadala" which connect all the parts of the roof of the Royal Audience hall to a single point.
