Wijayanthi Prasada was built as two stages.

The lower part of the building is built using brick and mortar while the upper storeys were reportedly wood. But with time, the wood has eroded away, leaving the support structure intact

The name Wijayanthi Prasada was inspired by the god Indra

Built for a king who was godlike

During the Polonnaruwa era, King Parakramabahu the first, or Parakramabahu the great was a king which was regarded to be one of the best to appear in ages. A status which the great king holds to-date.

The citadel which contains the Prasada is actually a construction by king Vijayabahu. But it was later destroyed by invaders. It was king Parakramabahu the first who built this building in the citadel along with a collection of other grand buildings and parks.
